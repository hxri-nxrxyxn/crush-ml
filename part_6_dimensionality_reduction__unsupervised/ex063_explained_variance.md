# ex063: Explained Variance Ratio

### Concept
The explained variance ratio tells you the proportion of the dataset’s variance that lies along each principal component.

### Usage
- If the first component explains 70% and the second explains 20%, you know that 90% of the information is contained in just two axes.
- **Choosing the right dimensions**: Instead of picking an arbitrary number of dimensions, it is better to choose the number of dimensions that add up to a sufficiently large portion of the variance (e.g., 95%).
